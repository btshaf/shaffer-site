# Case Study Development Skill

**Purpose**: Extract, structure, and develop compelling product management case studies with standardized format and animation opportunities.

**Target Output**: Complete case study content ready for implementation with identified visual/animation elements.

---

## Skill Framework

### Phase 1: Content Extraction
Systematic questioning to extract complete case study narrative

### Phase 2: Structure & Format
Organize content into standardized template with sections

### Phase 3: Visual Planning
Identify animation opportunities and visual elements needed

### Phase 4: Implementation Planning
Technical approach for case study page development

---

## Phase 1: Content Extraction Questions

### **A. Context & Foundation**

**Company & Market Context:**
1. What does [Company] do as a business? Core product/service?
2. Who are their main users/customers?
3. What was the competitive landscape when you joined?
4. Any relevant market dynamics or industry context?

**Your Role & Timeline:**
5. What was your specific title and scope of responsibility?
6. How long were you there? What was the project timeline?
7. What was the team structure around you?
8. Do you have any existing documents, specs, or materials from this project?

### **B. Problem Definition**

**The Core Problem:**
9. What was the main challenge you were brought in to solve?
10. How did you first discover or validate this problem?
11. What made this problem particularly urgent/important for the business?
12. Who were the key stakeholders affected by this problem?

**Existing State:**
13. What solutions (if any) were already in place?
14. What was the user experience like before your intervention?
15. What data/evidence did you have about the problem's scope?
16. What constraints did you face (technical, resource, political)?

### **C. Approach & Methodology**

**Research & Discovery:**
17. How did you validate and scope the problem initially?
18. What research methods did you use? (user interviews, analytics, etc.)
19. What frameworks or methodologies guided your approach?
20. How did you prioritize different aspects of the problem?

**Solution Development:**
21. What was your hypothesis for solving this problem?
22. How did you generate and evaluate potential solutions?
23. What were the key trade-offs you had to make?
24. How did you get buy-in for your recommended approach?

**Implementation Process:**
25. What was your step-by-step execution plan?
26. How did you manage scope and timeline?
27. What tools or processes did you put in place?
28. How did you handle changes or obstacles during execution?

### **D. Results & Impact**

**Quantitative Outcomes:**
29. What were the key metrics that improved? (specific numbers/percentages)
30. How did you measure success initially vs. final outcomes?
31. What was the timeline for seeing results?
32. Any unexpected metric improvements or discoveries?

**Qualitative Impact:**
33. What user feedback did you receive?
34. How did this change the team's workflow or capabilities?
35. What organizational changes resulted from this work?
36. What did stakeholders say about the outcomes?

### **E. Learnings & Insights**

**Key Insight/Aha Moment:**
37. What was the biggest strategic realization from this project?
38. When did this insight occur? What triggered it?
39. How did this change your approach to the problem?
40. How has this insight influenced your work since?

**Personal & Team Learnings:**
41. What would you do differently if starting over?
42. What challenges did you face that weren't initially obvious?
43. What did this teach you about PM methodology or product strategy?
44. How did this experience change your perspective on similar problems?

## Phase 2: Standard Case Study Structure

### **Template Outline:**
```
1. TL;DR Summary (key problem, solution, impact)
2. Context (company, market, your role)
3. Problem (challenge definition, evidence, stakes)
4. Approach (methodology, process, key decisions)
5. Results (quantitative + qualitative impact)
6. Key Insight (strategic realization)
7. Learnings (what you'd do differently, broader lessons)
8. Next Case Study (navigation)
```

### **Content Guidelines:**
- **Voice**: Objective tone with authentic personal challenges included
- **Length**: Aim for 1500-2500 words per case study
- **Confidentiality**: Company names OK except InfiniteChoice (use "Company X")
- **Metrics**: Real numbers preferred, approximations acceptable if noted
- **Focus**: PM methodology and strategic thinking over tactical execution

## Phase 3: Visual Element Planning

### **Animation Opportunities:**
- **Metrics**: Counting animations for key numbers
- **Process Flows**: Step-by-step reveals
- **Before/After States**: Transition animations
- **User Journeys**: Progressive disclosure
- **Timeline Elements**: Scroll-triggered progression

### **Static Visual Elements:**
- **User Journey Maps**: Key touchpoints and pain points
- **Process Diagrams**: Decision trees, workflow maps
- **Interface Mockups**: Before/after screenshots, key screens
- **Data Visualizations**: Charts, graphs, comparison tables

### **Interactive Elements:**
- **Internal Navigation**: Jump-to-section links
- **Scroll Progress**: Visual progress indicator
- **Expandable Sections**: Detailed methodology, additional context
- **Hover States**: Additional information on key terms/concepts

## Phase 4: Implementation Planning

### **Technical Approach:**
- **Page Structure**: `/case-studies/[slug]` dynamic route
- **Animation Library**: Framer Motion for React animations
- **Responsive Design**: Mobile-first with desktop enhancements
- **Performance**: Lazy loading for images, optimized animations

### **Content Management:**
- **Markdown Support**: Rich content with embedded components
- **Asset Organization**: Images, diagrams in `/public/case-studies/[slug]/`
- **SEO Optimization**: Dynamic meta tags, structured data
- **Accessibility**: Proper headings, alt text, keyboard navigation

## Usage Instructions

### **For Content Development:**
1. Use Phase 1 questions systematically for each case study
2. Record answers in detail - aim for comprehensive first draft
3. Apply Phase 2 structure to organize content
4. Identify Phase 3 visual opportunities based on content
5. Plan Phase 4 implementation requirements

### **For Multiple Case Studies:**
1. Complete one case study fully as template
2. Extract patterns for standardized components
3. Iterate template based on learnings
4. Scale to remaining case studies

### **Quality Checklist:**
- [ ] Clear problem statement with business context
- [ ] Logical flow from problem → approach → results
- [ ] Specific, measurable outcomes included
- [ ] Strategic insight clearly articulated
- [ ] Authentic challenges and learnings included
- [ ] Visual opportunities identified for key concepts
- [ ] Technical implementation plan defined
